### Background

Specialized in computational structural biology, holding a Licentiate in Biochemistry (2008) and a Ph.D. in Biophysics (2014). My research focused on the structural dynamics of multidomain and intrinsically disordered proteins in solution using NMR spectroscopy. I bridged the gap between methodologies by coupling wet-lab execution (recombinant protein expression and high-purity purification) with advanced computational modeling. From 2015 to 2024, while in academia, I expanded my expertise into software engineering in Python, delivering critical open-source suites including the NIH-funded IDPConformerGenerator and the BioExcel-funded HADDOCK3. Parallel to my technical work, I served as a scientific advisor across numerous collaborations on protein structure and dynamics, while consistently mentoring and empowering students to thrive in their careers. I have authored +30 scientific publications and one book chapter.
